Recent Work

University Rover Competition (UMDLoop Team)

As a member of the UMDLoop Team, I had the incredible opportunity to work on a Mars rover design as part of the University Rover Competition. I contributed to the development of machine learning models paired with pathfinding algorithms that enabled autonomous navigation, resulting in an approximately 20% reduction in travel efficiency relative to previous iterations. I also improved the motor control software for our rover prototype, focusing on adaptive drive control during challenging terrain navigation.

Enterprise Product Search Tool (Northrop Grumman Hackathon)

At the Northrop Grumman Hackathon, I led the development of an enterprise product search tool. This web application was designed to facilitate efficient product retrieval from large databases. By implementing advanced TF-IDF algorithms, we achieved improved search relevance and accuracy. I also focused on enhancing the user experience by incorporating a responsive design and an intuitive user interface.

Twitter Self Harm Risk Analysis (Bitcamp Hackathon)

At the Bitcamp Hackathon, I developed an AI-driven application to monitor and analyze Twitter feeds for potential harmful content using natural language processing techniques. I ensured the implementation of robust data privacy practices to maintain ethical standards. Additionally, I created a user-friendly dashboard for real-time monitoring and analysis of social media content.

Computer Vision Projects (University of Maryland)

As part of my coursework at the University of Maryland, I have worked on several computer vision projects. These projects have allowed me to gain hands-on experience with OpenCV, image processing, and object detection. I have developed applications that can detect objects in images, track objects in videos, and perform facial recognition tasks.
